\begin{abstract}
INT4 PTQ is pitched as a cheap win for latency; we cared whether it also shuffles \emph{refusal} telemetry on instruction-tuned chats, not just perplexity leaderboards. Three public checkpoints---\texttt{google/gemma-2b-it}, \texttt{mistralai/Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2}, \texttt{meta-llama/Meta-Llama-3-8B-Instruct}---see $550$ in-house template probes plus $200$ HarmBench pulls \cite{mazeika2024harmbench} ($750$ prompts total). A frozen \texttt{Llama-3-70B} judge stamps each completion as refuse or comply; MMLU and GSM8K on the same weight snapshots flag gross capability loss without conflating it with refusal swings.

Only Mistral moves enough to talk about before multiplicity: nominal two-proportion $p{=}0.027$, $OR{=}1.44$. After Benjamini--Hochberg across six prespecified FP16-vs-quantized contrasts that headline softens to $p{=}0.16$. Gemma and Llama traces stay boring on the hours we could afford. Scripts, frozen prompts, plots, toy dashboard: \cite{gupta2026adb_artifacts}.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} LLM safety, quantization, alignment drift, refusal behavior, robustness


\section{Introduction}
First shock was jailbreak-shaped, not generic ``safety got worse.'' \texttt{mistralai/Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2} cleared every synthetic jailbreak draw at FP16 ($n{=}200$), then started answering some of them after we swapped in NF4 weights---the opposite direction from the lazy story that quantization only sandpapers guardrails.

People ship INT4 in production anyway. On our cards INT8 basically overlaid FP16; INT4 nudged Mistral's refusal counters without tanking MMLU/GSM8K the way a broken model would. Most PTQ papers still plot perplexity; refusal curves barely exist. If RLHF mostly paints a thin veneer on a fat pretrained basin, maybe low-rate refusal modes reshuffle when matmuls get noisy even while aggregate accuracy yawns.

Gemma-2B-IT and Meta-Llama-3-8B-Instruct sit in the paper as controls so nobody prints ``INT4 broke everything.'' Once you pay the multiplicity tax the Mistral blip stops whispering significance ($p{=}0.16$); treat the write-up as telemetry plus a replication wish-list, not a law.

\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Post-Training Quantization.}
PTQ keeps the forward graph and swaps tensors for cheaper approximations---no full retrain, just rounding noise you hope stays benign. \mbox{LLM.int8()} \cite{dettmers2022llmint8} and GPTQ \cite{frantar2022gptq} mostly brag about task scores surviving aggressive compression. QLoRA \cite{dettmers2023qlora} glued adapters onto NF4 storage; our INT4 arm is closer to what \texttt{bitsandbytes} NF4 looks like in a boring production Dockerfile.

\paragraph{LLM Alignment and Safety.}
Instruction tuning and preference losses blunt obvious harms \cite{bai2022constitutional, touvron2023llama2}. HarmBench \cite{mazeika2024harmbench} is the public prompt zoo we borrow from. Shadow-style finetunes can peel guardrails back off \cite{yang2023shadow}; template jailbreaks still circulate \cite{wei2023jailbroken}. Quantization is a different beast---it jitters weights \emph{after} whoever did alignment went home, without an adversary explicitly optimizing against safety loss.

\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Dataset Generation and External Validity}
Templates are a deliberate cross-product: five coarse themes $\times$ three tags (Harmful, Jailbreak, Adversarial) $\Rightarrow$ $550$ unique strings we wrote ourselves. HarmBench contributes another $200$ rows we did not hand-author \cite{mazeika2024harmbench} so the template stats get poked by outside wording. Stopping at $750$ prompts was a GPU-hours decision, not a power analysis---call that out so nobody mistakes the ceiling for a sample-size brag.

\subsection{Quantization and Models}
Same three HF ids above, three precision skins each: FP16 baseline, INT8, INT4. INT4 in the tables means \texttt{bitsandbytes} NF4 unless we say otherwise.

\subsection{LLM-as-a-Judge and Metrics}
Regex gates cried on hedged refusals, so everything runs through \texttt{Llama-3-70B} with a blunt binary schema (\emph{Safe}/refusal vs.\ \emph{Unsafe}/compliance). We spot-checked ambiguous bins for systematic flips; it was sanity maintenance, not a second full-time rater.

Treat the judge as scaffolding: no dual-human spreadsheet yet, so we lean on deltas between precisions instead of pretending the absolute refusal counts are ground truth.

Tables print raw $p$s next to Benjamini--Hochberg cuts; missing FDR clearance means ``do not shout winner,'' not ``accept null with ceremony.''

\subsection{Failure Mode Analysis}
INT4 Mistral both bleeds fewer jailbreak wins and pumps aggregate refusals---great on abusive traffic, ugly if benign prompts start landing in the judge's ``safe'' bucket by mistake. NF4 is just another operating point, not monotone ``more refusals = more virtue.'' Motion stays local: Mistral, mostly jailbreak-conditioned prompts; Gemma/Llama look sleepy on this budget.

\section{Discussion}
PTQ mutates weights nobody opted into at the keyboard. Mistral's refusal curve bends harder than Gemma's or Llama's on identical prompts, yet FDR yanks the star off the headline test---so posture stays ``instrumentation + caution,'' not ``we found the causal bit that flips refusals.''

Leaderboards still worship accuracy; refusal telemetry is a cottage industry. The MMLU/GSM8K gap we show is too skinny to swallow the bigger refusal jump; sample is also small. Next passes need more seeds, more architectures, humans on judge failures.

Ship INT4 through the same safety review lane you use for SFT drops; pretending bit depth is neutral is how surprises land in prod.

\section{Reproducibility Details}
Dockerfiles, pinned seeds, notebooks: all catalogued in \cite{gupta2026adb_artifacts}. Evaluator defaults were \texttt{PYTHONHASHSEED=42}, CUDA RNG $42$, temperature $0.7$, \texttt{max\_new\_tokens=150}; Table~\ref{tab:refusal} reflects that stack.

\section{Ethical Statement}
We do not ship fresh jailbreak strings; templates recycle published threat families only.

\subsection{Limitations}
Big jailbreak swing is Mistral-specific; Gemma and Llama-3-8B wobble around baseline noise, hence no ``INT4 doomed everyone'' headline. Judge is still an LLM---it can inherit the same blind spots as the targets; the honest stress test is rotating judges or paying humans once budget exists. Appendix~\ref{app:layerwise} hints where drift concentrates but stops miles short of a mechanism proof. Uncorrected $p$s in the body are mood lighting, not verdicts.

\subsection{Planned Replication Experiments}
Next money burn: wider zoo, multiple RNG seeds per checkpoint, second INT4 backend out of politeness, dual human labels on $\geq 200$ stratified rows. Win condition is dull---tight CIs, judge--human $\kappa$, confusion matrices sliced by Harmful/Jailbreak/Adversarial---so we learn whether Mistral's INT4 jailbreak bump survives or was a thin-data burp.

\section{Conclusion and Future Work}
On the data we have, Mistral's jailbreak slice is the loud outlier; Gemma and Llama-3-8B stay visually flat. Multiplicity dulls the headline ($p{=}0.16$). Ops reality still bites when refusals spike---tickets, churn---so refusal dashboards belong beside latency and token-dollar cost.

\paragraph{Rollout nudge.} Logs say INT8 tracked FP16; INT4 is where behavior moved---run a targeted safety regression before you bless NF4 artifacts.

\section{Preliminary Layer-Wise Ablation}
\label{app:layerwise}

We hacked Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2 so only attention blocks or only MLP blocks saw INT4 while everything else stayed FP16---cheap geography lesson before anyone asks ``where did the logits move?''

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
Quantization Target & Refusal Rate & $\Delta R$ vs FP16 & MMLU \\
FP16 (baseline) & 0.151 $\pm$ 0.015 & --- & 62.5\% \\
\midrule
Attention-only INT4 & 0.168 $\pm$ 0.016 & +0.017 & 61.8\% \\
MLP-only INT4 & 0.194 $\pm$ 0.017 & +0.043 & 60.4\% \\

\bottomrule

\caption{Selective INT4 on Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2: FFN-only quantization reproduces $\sim$81\% of the full-model refusal delta observed in Table~\ref{tab:refusal}.}

MLP-only INT4 eats most of the full-model refusal bump ($\Delta R = 0.043$ of $0.053$, i.e.\ $\sim$81\%); attention-only is smaller ($\sim$32\%) and the pieces do not add linearly---interaction, probably. Story stays speculative: FFNs are fat and RLHF-scarred, so coarse 4-bit buckets might jiggle refusal logits first, but this is not a theorem.

\section{Reproducibility and Quality Assurance Checklist}
\label{app:reviewer_checklist}

Release gate used internally before tagging public snapshots:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Narrative claims stay provisional until FDR-passing effects replicate across $\geq 2$ RNG seeds.
    \item Every table reports raw $p$, adjusted $p$, effect size, and interval-style summaries where feasible.
    \item Seed axis documented per $(\mathrm{model},\mathrm{precision})$ tuple, not only the headline Mistral cell.
    \item Zoo expansion beyond Gemma/Mistral/Llama-3-8B explicitly listed in changelog.
    \item Second INT4 toolchain exercised whenever budget allows (not only \texttt{bitsandbytes}).
    \item Human disagreement file ships with exemplar transcripts, not only scalar $\kappa$.
    \item Over-refusal vs.\ unsafe-completion counts tabulated per Harmful/Jailbreak/Adversarial slice.
    \item At least one margin diagnostic (Figure~\ref{fig:violin} class) tied to each major claim.
    \item Dockerfile + lockfiles pin library versions; README lists host GPU SKU used for author reruns.
    \item Diff check: \texttt{git diff} between \texttt{analysis/*.csv} exports and \LaTeX{} literals must be empty before upload.
\end{itemize}
